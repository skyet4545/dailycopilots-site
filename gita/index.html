What does Krishna mean in 2.47 by “you have a right to action, never to its fruits”?
Karma-yoga. Krishna is not telling Arjuna to stop caring about outcomes — he is telling him to stop letting the outcome own the action. Do your duty (svadharma) fully and skillfully, but release attachment to the results, which are never wholly in your control. This is how action becomes free of anxiety and of the karmic binding that comes from craving reward.
