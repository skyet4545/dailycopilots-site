Questions before you download

Honest answers to the ones everyone asks.

Is this just ChatGPT with a Bible theme?
No. ChatGPT answers from memory and will paraphrase scripture without verse numbers. Bible Copilot is built around a strict verse-anchoring discipline — every response cites the actual reference text, not a paraphrase from training data. The six study modes enforce a specific analytical lens (observation, interpretation, theology, application, apologetics) that a generic chatbot has no mechanism to apply.
What if it gives me bad theology?
On contested doctrines — Calvinism vs. Arminianism, eschatology, baptism mode — the app presents the major positions held by serious Christians across denominations, without advocating for one. On historical-factual claims, responses cite scripture by verse number. You and your pastor are still the authority; this is a study tool, not a creed.
I already have YouVersion. Why do I need this?
Bible Copilot is not a reading app — it’s the understanding app you open after you finish the chapter. They’re designed to coexist. YouVersion excels at reading plans and devotionals. Bible Copilot answers the question that reading plans leave open: “What does this verse actually mean?”
Does it know my translation (KJV, NIV, ESV, NASB)?
Yes. Verse text is pulled from an API serving multiple translations; KJV is the default. You can reference any translation-standard verse and the app will work with it.
I’m not a Bible scholar. Will this be over my head?
Three of the five App Store reviewers said the same thing — one verbatim: “I don’t consider myself a bible scholar by any stretch.” The Summary and Apply modes are designed exactly for non-scholars who want depth without the jargon. Interpret and Theology modes go deeper when you’re ready.
What happens to my data? Is it used to train AI models?
No. Your study questions and journal entries are not used to train any AI model. The app calls an AI inference API per request; your content is not retained by the model provider for training.
Can I cancel after the free trial?
Yes. Subscriptions are managed entirely in your Apple Settings (Settings > Apple ID > Subscriptions). No support email required. Cancel before day 8 of the annual trial and you are not charged.
How is $29.99/year fair for a Bible app?
It’s roughly $2.49/month — less than a single paperback commentary, and you get unlimited questions every day. The free tier (15 questions/day) is enough for most daily devotional readers at no cost. The annual plan is for readers who want to go deep regularly.
